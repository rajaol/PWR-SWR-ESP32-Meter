Power CalculationThe firmware utilizes the Power-to-Voltage relationship of a rectified directional coupler. Since $P = \frac{V^2}{R}$, and the bridge provides a linear voltage relative to the RF amplitude, we apply a scaling constant (K-Factor):$$P = K \cdot (V_{fwd} + V_{diode})^2$$K-Factor (12.40): Maps the bridge’s DC output to Watts based on a $20\text{W}$ at $1.27\text{V}$ reference.Diode Compensation (0.17V): Corrects for the forward voltage drop of Schottky/Germanium diodes, ensuring accuracy at low power.2. SWR CalculationStanding Wave Ratio is derived from the Reflection Coefficient ($\Gamma$):$$\Gamma = \frac{V_{ref} + V_{diode}}{V_{fwd} + V_{diode}}$$$$SWR = \frac{1 + \Gamma}{1 - \Gamma}$$The system samples 60 times per loop and holds the highest detected value for 3 seconds to capture SSB voice peaks.⚙️ Calibration Procedure50Ω Match: Connect a 50Ω dummy load; $V_{ref}$ should be $0.00\text{V}$, resulting in SWR 1.0.100Ω Mismatch: Connect a 100Ω load. The meter must show SWR 2.00.If SWR < 2.0: Increase diode_vf.If SWR > 2.0: Decrease diode_vf.No Load (Open Circuit): Transmit briefly with no antenna. $V_{fwd}$ and $V_{ref}$ will equalize, triggering the HIGH SWR Red Alert.📡 SWR in HAM Radio CommunicationsIn HF (High Frequency) communications, SWR measures impedance matching between the radio and the antenna system. Most modern transceivers require a 50Ω environment. When impedance deviates, energy is reflected back to the source.While an SWR of 1.5:1 is ideal, ratios above 3:1 are hazardous. High SWR causes excessive heat in the Final PA (Power Amplifier) transistors, potentially leading to catastrophic hardware failure. This digital monitor’s High SWR Alert and 3-second peak hold provide a critical safety margin by visualizing transients that traditional analog needles often miss during rapid voice transmissions.
